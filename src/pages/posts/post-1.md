I want this blog to be a real reflection of my journey in life insurance, not just the polished parts. Behind the license, the job title, and the progress people see now, there were many moments where I questioned myself, felt discouraged, and wondered if I was really cut out for this path.

## How it started

When I was first hired, I was excited but also nervous. Life insurance was a completely new world to me. I knew this opportunity could open doors for me, but I also knew it would require discipline, confidence, and a lot of learning. At the beginning, everything felt unfamiliar — the terminology, the policies, the responsibility, and the pressure of needing to pass my state exams.

## Failing the life and health exams

One of the hardest parts of my journey was failing the state exams for life and health multiple times. Every failure hit me emotionally because I wanted it so badly. I felt embarrassed, frustrated, and disappointed in myself. It is hard to explain how defeating it feels to study, hope, show up, and still not get the result you prayed for.

But those failures taught me something important: wanting something is not always enough by itself. Sometimes you have to keep showing up even when it feels humiliating, even when it feels slow, and even when other people might not see how hard you are trying.

## What changed for me

Eventually, I stopped looking at each failed attempt as proof that I could not do it. I started seeing each attempt as part of the process. I kept studying. I kept learning. I kept trying. And in the middle of all that adversity, I built resilience.

Passing was not just about getting a license. It became personal. It meant proving to myself that I could push through discouragement and still finish what I started.

## Finally getting licensed

When I finally passed and became licensed, it meant more to me because of everything it took to get there. It was not an overnight success story. It was a story built on persistence, trial, adversity, and faith. That moment reminded me that delays do not always mean denial. Sometimes they are just part of becoming stronger.

## Why I’m sharing this

I want this blog to document the real side of the journey. Not just sales, not just wins, and not just titles. I want it to reflect the growth that happens behind the scenes — the pressure, the self-doubt, the small breakthroughs, and the strength it takes to keep going.

If you are in a season where progress feels slow, I hope my story reminds you that failing is not the end. Sometimes it is just part of the road to becoming who you are meant to be.

> My journey into life insurance was not perfect, but it was real — and that is exactly why it matters.

## What I learned

- Growth takes patience
- Failure does not mean you are finished
- Confidence can be built through repetition
- Persistence matters more than perfection
- Adversity can shape your purpose
